Enrichment analyses¶

Single¶

We first examine the univariate enrichment of SCZ risk variants in ASoC SNPs, OCRs and other annotated genomic regions, respectively, by using TORUS. iN-Glut ASoC variants showed over 100-fold remarkable enrichment for SZ risk variants, while NPC ASoC variants showed ~66-fold enrichment. In contrast, both iN-Glut and NPC OCRs revealed only ~3-fold enrichments (Fig 1). ASoC variants were more enriched for genetic variants associated with SCZ than their corresponding OCRs, introducing a powerful framework to identify SCZ causal variants.

It has been widely proven that SCZ risk loci are enriched in non-coding regulatory regions (1). We verified that conserved and 3'UTR sequences were slightly more enriched for SCZ risk variants than coding regions. Furthermore, SCZ risk variants were also highly enriched in 5'UTR and promoter sequences. We further performed single-annotation analysis using brain eQTL for comparison, since it has been demonstrated that aggregate associations between brain eQTLs and many brain disorder GWAS variants, such as SCZ (2). We discovered that brain eQTL revealed ~4-fold enrichment for SCZ risk variants (Fig 1).
